Sukhasana, also known as the Easy Pose or Pleasant Pose, is a simple seated yoga pose that is commonly practiced for meditation and pranayama (breath control exercises). In Sanskrit, "sukha" means "ease" or "comfort," and "asana" means "pose" or "posture."

To practice Sukhasana:

1.Sit on the floor or on a cushion with your legs crossed. If sitting on the floor is uncomfortable, you can sit on a folded blanket or yoga block.
2.Place your hands on your knees or in your lap.
3.Lengthen your spine, keeping your back straight but not rigid. Relax your shoulders away from your ears.
4.Close your eyes and focus on your breath or engage in meditation.
