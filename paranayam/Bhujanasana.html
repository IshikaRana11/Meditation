Bhujangasana, also known as Cobra Pose, is a foundational yoga pose that involves arching the back while lying prone on the ground. In Sanskrit, "Bhujanga" means "cobra," and "asana" means "pose" or "posture." Bhujangasana is a beginner-friendly backbend that offers a variety of physical and mental benefits


To practice Dhanurasana:

1. Lie down on your stomach (prone position) on a yoga mat.
2. Place your palms on the ground under your shoulders, elbows close to your body, and fingers pointing forward.
3.Press the tops of your feet and thighs into the mat..
4.Inhale and slowly lift your chest and head off the mat, keeping your elbows slightly bent.
5.Lengthen your spine and open your chest, lifting through the crown of your head.
6.Keep your shoulders relaxed away from your ears and gaze forward or slightly upward.
7.Hold the pose for a few breaths, breathing deeply and evenly.
8.Exhale and gently release the pose, lowering your chest and head back to the mat
