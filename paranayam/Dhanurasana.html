Dhanurasana, also known as the Bow Pose, is a yoga asana that resembles an archer's bow when performed. In Sanskrit, "Dhanu" means "bow," and "asana" means "pose" or "posture." Dhanurasana is considered an intermediate-level yoga pose and offers a range of physical and mental benefits.


To practice Dhanurasana:

1. Lie down on your stomach (prone position) on a yoga mat.
2. Bend your knees and bring your heels close to your buttocks.
3.Reach back with your hands and hold onto your ankles.
4.Inhale deeply and lift your chest, head, and thighs off the mat by pulling your ankles upward.
5.Keep your gaze forward and your shoulders relaxed.
6.Hold the pose for a few breaths, maintaining the arch in your back.
7.Exhale and gently release the pose, lowering your chest, head, and thighs back to the mat.
