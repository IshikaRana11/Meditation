Balasana, also known as Child's Pose, is a calming and restorative yoga pose that gently stretches the spine, hips, thighs, and ankles. In Sanskrit, "Bala" means "child," and "asana" means "pose" or "posture." Balasana is often practiced as a resting pose or a counterpose to more vigorous yoga sequences, providing a sense of surrender and relaxation.


To practice Dhanurasana:
1. Kneel on the floor with your big toes touching and your knees slightly wider than hip-width apart.
2.Sit back on your heels, then exhale and lower your torso forward between your thighs.
3.Lengthen your spine and stretch your arms forward, placing your palms on the mat or resting them on the floor beside your feet.
4.Rest your forehead on the mat or a yoga block if it doesn't comfortably reach the floor.
5.Allow your hips to sink towards your heels and relax your shoulders away from your ears.
6.Close your eyes and focus on your breath, breathing deeply and evenly into your back body.
7.Hold the pose for several breaths or as long as feels comfortable.
